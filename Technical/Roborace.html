Robo Race Competition Rulebook

1. Event Overview

Robo Race is a competitive race where participants design and build bots that navigate a track full of turns, speed zones, and obstacles. The goal is to complete the course in the shortest possible time, with precision and stability.

2. Race Format

  • Type: Time Trial / Knockout / Obstacle Race
  • Track Type: Indoor/Outdoor with curves, slopes, bridges, or ramps
  • Match Duration: Max 5 minutes per round (may vary)

3. Robot Specifications

3.1 Weight

  • Max Weight: 3.5 kg Â±10% tolerance
  • Hard Limit: 3.85 kg

3.2 Dimensions

  • Length: Max 40 cm
  • Width: Max 35 cm
  • Height: Max 30 cm
  • (Bot should fit inside a 40x35 cm box)

3.3 Power Supply

  • Only onboard power supply (no external wires)
  • Allowed: Li-ion / LiPo / NiMH batteries
  • Max Voltage: 12V

3.4 Control

  • Manual Mode: Wireless control via RF, Bluetooth, or Wi-Fi
  • Autonomous Mode (Optional): Sensor-based navigation
  • Failsafe switch required for both modes

3.5 Mobility

  • Wheeled bots only (2WD, 4WD, differential drive allowed)
  • Tracks or legs not allowed unless permitted
  • Max speed should be controllable on the track

4. Track Details

Made of plywood, plastic sheets, or cardboard. Includes:

  • Curves and Zig-Zags
  • Speed Breakers
  • Ramps and Slopes
  • Checkpoints

5. Race Rules

5.1 Starting Procedure

  • Bot must start behind the starting line
  • Timer starts once the bot moves
  • Timer stops when it crosses the finish line

5.2 Victory Conditions

  • Fastest completion time wins
  • In case of tie: fewer penalties decide winner

5.3 Penalties

  • Going off track: External assistance during run: +15 seconds

6. Team Guidelines

  • Max 3 members per team
  • One bot per team
  • One operator must be declared before the run

7. Disqualification

  • Bot exceeds weight or size limit
  • Wireless interference with other teams
  • Unsafe design or exposed wires
  • Damaging the arena or track
  • Unsportsmanlike behavior

8. Safety Rules

  • Bot must have a proper enclosure
  • Batteries must be secured and fire-safe
  • No sharp edges or loose parts
  • All bots must pass a tech inspection

9. Scoring & Prizes

  • Based on Time
