  • QR Code Scavenger Hunt Rulebook
    • 1. Team Composition
      • Each team must consist of 1 to 5 members.
      • Only one device (smartphone) per team can be used for scanning QR codes.
      • Members must remain together at all times during the event.
    • 2. Event Objective
      • Navigate through the college campus by scanning QR codes hidden in various locations.
      • Solve the clues, riddles, technical puzzles, or complete tasks to reach the next QR code.
      • The team that solves all clues in the shortest time with the highest score wins.
    • 3. Equipment Required
      • At least one smartphone with:
        • Camera and QR code scanner (or in-built scanner).
        • Internet access.
      • Optional: Pen and notebook for solving riddles/codes.
    • 4. Game Format
      • Starting Point:
        • All teams will start from a common location declared on the event day.
      • Flow:
        • A QR code will be provided to each team at the starting point.
        • Scanning the QR will open a clue/task (text, image, riddle, code challenge, etc.).
        • Solving the clue reveals the location of the next QR code.
        • This continues for a fixed number of stages (e.g., 7–10).
      • Clue Types May Include:
        • Logical or mathematical riddles
        • Tech trivia
        • Mini-coding puzzles
        • Campus-based references
    • 6. Time and Scoring System
      • Time Limit:
        • Maximum duration: 60 minutes
        • Event ends when all teams complete the hunt or the time runs out.
      • Scoring Criteria:
        • Each QR clue solved: +10 pts
        • Early Completion Bonus (Top 3 teams): +30/+20/+10 pts
        • Requesting a Hint (per clue): -5 pts
    • 7. Rules & Regulations
      • General Rules:
        • All team members must stay together during the event.
        • Only one team member may scan the QR code at each checkpoint.
        • QR codes must not be removed, damaged, or shared with other teams.
        • Each QR code is unique to its stage—no skipping allowed.
        • Teams must respect college property and follow staff instructions.
      • Technical Tasks:
        • Use only your device for online lookups or solving challenges.
        • Avoid using external help from other students or teams.
    • 8. Grounds for Disqualification
      • Tampering with or destroying QR codes.
      • Interfering with other teams or revealing clues.
      • Splitting the team or using multiple phones for unfair advantage.
      • Misconduct with volunteers or violation of campus discipline.
